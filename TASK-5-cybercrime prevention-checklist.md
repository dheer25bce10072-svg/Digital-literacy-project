Financial & UPI Safety 💳
Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone under any circumstances. Remember that receiving money never requires entering OTP - this is the most common scam targeting college students in India. If someone asks for your PIN claiming to send you money, it's definitely a scam. Always verify QR codes before scanning them. Scammers often send payment QR codes pretending you'll receive money, especially on platforms like OLX, Quikr, and during fake refund calls.

Check your bank statements and transaction history regularly. Set up instant SMS alerts for all transactions so you notice unauthorized activity immediately. If you spot any suspicious transaction, report it to your bank within 3 days to increase chances of recovery. Never click on payment links received via WhatsApp or SMS from unknown numbers, even if they look official.

Password & Account Security 🔐
Create strong, unique passwords for every account using a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols. Avoid using birthdays, names, or common words that can be easily guessed. Never reuse the same password across different websites because if one account gets hacked, all your accounts become vulnerable.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts including email, social media, banking apps, and college portals. This adds an extra security layer even if someone steals your password. Your email is the master key to all other accounts since password resets go there, so protect it with your strongest password. Always log out completely from shared computers in college labs, libraries, or cyber cafes.

Social Media & Privacy 📱
Limit personal information shared publicly online. Avoid posting phone numbers, email addresses, home addresses, real-time locations, or hostel room details. Don't share travel plans publicly like "Going home for 2 weeks!" as this signals your absence. Make your Instagram and Facebook profiles private and review your followers list regularly.

Be cautious about accepting friend requests from strangers with incomplete profiles, stock photos, or no mutual connections. Fake profiles are often used to collect information or run romance scams. Think before posting anything - once online, it's permanent even after deletion. Future employers often check social media profiles.

Online Safety & Scam Prevention 🌐
Verify links before clicking by hovering your mouse to see the actual destination URL. Watch for URL misspellings like "amazom.com" or "paytmm.com" which indicate phishing websites. Be extremely cautious of job offers requiring upfront payment, registration fees, or training charges - legitimate companies never ask for money from applicants.

Avoid using public WiFi networks in cafes, malls, or airports for banking or entering passwords. Use mobile data or VPN for sensitive transactions. Keep your apps, phone software, and laptop operating system updated regularly since updates contain critical security patches.

🚨 Report Cybercrime Immediately
National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in

Helpline: 1930 (Toll-free, Available 24/7)

Report incidents within 24 hours for the best chance of fund recovery. Save all evidence including screenshots, transaction IDs, phone numbers, and chat history before filing your complaint. Also inform your bank immediately if financial fraud has occurred.
